Share of Voice in the Olden Days

Originally

Share of voice was simply a measure of how many of all the ads were yours.

\[ \frac{\text{Your No. of Ads}}{\text{Total No. of Ads}} = \text{Share of Voice} \]

If you pay for one ad and the website rotates ten ads ...
well, then ten percent of visitors will se your ad.

\[ \frac{\text{1}}{\text{10}} = \text{10%} \]

What you pay is what you get. Simple.

These Days

"Share of voice" is any number of things. For example:

  1. Share of Social Media Mentions
  2. Share of News Mentions
  3. Share of Google Searches

What these have in common are that 1) you don't decide your own share, and 2) the voice, the denominator, is fluid and often unknown. The world decides for you. And it changes. All. The. Time.

Examples of Fluidity

1. The Demographic Baseline Changes

  • Number of users grows/declines in general/specific markets
  • Demographic changes of platform (geeks --> politicians --> celebrities)

2. The Pool of "Total" Data Changes

  • The sample changes (decahose vs firehose)
  • Adding/removing sources of news etc.
  • Old Instagram dataset at Brandwatch: pool of users' queries

3. The API Changes (soon Instagram - pooled data not allowed)

4. The algorithms change or their precision changes

A Few Words on Keywords

The more comprehensive and concise the list of words, the more accurately you measure. Simple as that.

  • Remember to include specificities in different markets (rice+cassava+mandioca)
  • Add more languages
  • Remember different versions (program/programme)
  • Remember different tenses (distributed/distribute)
  • Remember both positive and negative versions (food security/insecurity)?
  • Remember different genders in gendered languages
  • Include un-accented versions as well (distribuée/distribuee)
  • Include common speling errors (<--)

To Sum Up

There are many different shares of voices that will make sense to KPIs.

\[ \frac{\text{No. of WFP mentions}}{\text{Total no. of mentions of WFP and similar agencies}} = \text{Share of voice} \]

\[ \frac{\text{No. of WFP mentions}}{\text{Total no. of mentions about logisics in Yemen}} = \text{Share of voice} \]

\[ \frac{\text{No. of successful delivery stories}}{\text{Total no. of WFP mentions}} = \text{Share of voice} \]

And many many more. Your job is to find those that make most sense to your KPIs and narratives.

Bonus: Google Searches

Let's also look at one last thing that's really interesting, and almost a share of voice. Google searches. How much do pelople search for WFP?

\[ \frac{\text{No. of WFP searches}}{\text{Total no. of Google searches}} = \text{Share of voice} \]

Only, we don't know

  1. No. of WFP searches
  2. Total no. of searches

So that's not possible. Oh well.

Luckily, there's Google Trends

Although we don't get individual Shares of Voice, we do get share of voice compared to the highest share of voice. The higest number of searches was for WHO in April 2018, and the search for other organisations is then relative to that. In that month, WFP's share of voice relative to WHO was only 7%.
